About

David, a wayward computational physicist.

David is a computational physicist, with aspirations of becoming a scientific programmer, game designer, machine learning expert, or just a guy who likes pretending to be a Viking. He first fell in love with physics at the ripe old age of 6 years old, after a trip to the Parkes Radio Telescope. Since then, he has strived to become a “Doctor in Space”, but has so far only managed the “Doctor” part, finishing his PhD in Theoretical Computational Quantum Physics at RMIT in 2018.

David has a love for programming, enjoying the challenge and learning aspect that comes with every new task. He also enjoys learning various aspects of everything, and has worked professionally in biological fields, and has a keen interest in hands-on experimental work. Full of grandiose ideas, the ever-growing LIST is a testament to his grand ideas, even if few of them rarely see the light of day.

He also designed and coded a website for an international research group, and programmed a Labjack microcontroller to modernise a piece of 20-year-old equipment.

In his spare time, he enjoys Parkour, pretending to be a Viking, and blacksmithing, amongst many other things.
