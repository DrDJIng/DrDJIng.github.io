Bioinformatics with Rosalind - DNA

July 23, 2020

1 minute read

 LinkedIn

 Email

There’s an offshoot of data science called Bioinformatics, and it’s essentially the study of how to extract meaningful information out of genetic information. Now, this sounds super cool, so I’ve decided to learn a bit about it. To help me with this, I’ll be doing the Rosalind problems, and posting my solution here.

The first problem is to do with DNA, and it’s a relatively simple one. All DNA (that I know of) is made up of four nucleotides attached in sequence, along with some deoxyribose sugars and some phosphate groups. The four nucleotides are (A)denine, (C)ytosine, (T)hymine, and (G)uanine. And so the first Rosalind challenge:

Given: A DNA string s of length at most 1000 nucleotides.

Return: Four integers (separated by spaces) counting the respective number of times that the symbols ‘A’, ‘C’, ‘G’, and ‘T’ occur in s.

My solution follows:
