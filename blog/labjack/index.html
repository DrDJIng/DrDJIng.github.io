Labjack experiments

March 6, 2020

2 minute read

 LinkedIn

 Email

My current job has me do a lot of random bits and pieces, very little coherent “This is your job” tasks. To date, I’ve created a website [LINK], studied Machine Learning, decided it was overkill for the image recognition program I then created, and helped to write a paper or two. Now, however, I am stepping well outside of my comfort area and attempting to modernize the interface between a computer and a two-decade old piece of lab equipment.

To this end, I researcher a few options, and settled on the LabJack, mostly because it was cheap and I am sure I can write controller code for it. Thankfully, it comes with drivers and a ready-to-use code-base of high level functions in a variety of languages. Since I’m trying to get out of my MATLAB rut and because I want someone else to be able to modify the code in the future, I chose to use Python 3.8.

Since I’ve only recently decided to do this blog, I’ve actually made a decent bit of progress that, unfortunately, isn’t documented in blog form. I’m just going to start from where I am, and give a brief rundown of what I did up to this point. Most of these I achieved thanks to the excellent examples packaged with the Python functions by the LabJack team.

  • How to set a voltage on the Analog Outputs
  • How to stream data from the Labjack at rates up to 50kHz, with variable resolution
  • How to plot live-updating graphs

I still have a long way to go, and less than a month to finish this project. My to-do list includes multi-threading, adding an arbitrary “waveform” generator option, data output, testing and verification, feedback, all that jazz. This is going to be a hell of a month!
