Idle clicker game

November 6, 2020

3 minute read

 LinkedIn

 Email

For my first full game, I’ve decided to do an idle clicker. You know, like Cookie Clicker but with a different theme. Given 2020, and my unemployment, I’ve decided to make it a job-search themed clicker.

The basic idea is that every click is a job application. As more and more applications are sent out, the rejections start rolling in. Motivation builds as your progress, which lets you buy “auto-clickers” which will automatically apply for jobs for you. The progression starts off relatively normal, and then extends into absurdity.

I’m making this in Godot as I have more experience with Python, which GDScript is based on.

So the gameplay loop is simple. Click button -> get rejection -> gain motivation, which departs from reality already, but I thought motivation was more useful as a spending resource than depression! At this point, 1 application leads to 1 rejection which gives 1 motivation. While the application:rejection ratio will always remain 1:1, I may tweak the motivation output.

I’ve decided to implement 11 stages at this point, and I’ll list them here:

  1. Click button, for a 1 to 1 ratio of click to rejection.
  2. Write script, to automatically apply for jobs for you!
  3. Add a job board, find a job board and find more jobs to be rejected from!
  4. Talk to a recruiter, recruiters are an excellent source of jobs to apply for!
  5. Go to a jobs fair, lots and lots of jobs there.
  6. Do an internship, it’s unpaid but who cares at this point?
  7. Alchemy, try to turn gold into jobs!
  8. Large Job Collider, try and find the Jobs field!
  9. Create another universe, try to find one where you already have a job!
  10. Try magic, it’s the only way to get a job these days!

The coding itself is relatively simple. Godot provides a number of typical built in functions to provide game functionality, and here we mostly want to exploit the _process(delta) function. This function runs every time step, which is based on your framerate. Delta is the length of time between each step. For example, at 60 FPS delta would be equal to 1/60 or approximately 16.7 ms.

I’ve decided to go a route where I set a rate of gain for each option, which can then be modified by external factors (such as achievements when I add them). Alexander King wrote an excellent post on the math behind the ideal growth rate for cost and return. Following his advice, I’ve chosen a growth rate of 1.09, such that the cost rate goes as

$$C(N) = C_0 C_G ^ N$$

where (C(N)) is the cost function, (C_G) is the cost growth, and (N) is the number already purchased.

Alright, so I have the basic idea of the game all set. Now I just need assets and music! Well, that’s really unfortunate, because my creative abilities in those areas are sorely lacking.

I put together some amazing pictures by myself in GIMP in all of a handful of seconds. Hopefully I can find someone to pay to do a better job! For music, I’ve found some royalty free songs that I can use, courtesy of One Man Symphony.

Add in some transition effects, and viola! An idle clicker game! There is still a lot I would like to add in, including an intro cinematic, serialization, achievements, a new game + mode, and others.

For the moment, though, I’ll just live a secret double life that is actually just my normal life: sending out swathes of applications!

The game will be available somewhere at somepoint.
